Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about House Wellness checkups.

About the Service

What exactly is a House Wellness checkup?
It's a 112-point visual inspection of your home's major systems—roof, HVAC, plumbing, electrical, foundation, and more. We document everything with photos and deliver a prioritized report within 48 hours. Think of it as an annual physical for your home.
Is this a certified home inspection?
No, and that's intentional. Certified home inspections are designed for real estate transactions and typically cost $500–700. House Wellness is a proactive maintenance checkup for homeowners who already own their home and want to stay ahead of problems. We're not licensed home inspectors—we're maintenance professionals.
How is this different from a home inspection for buying/selling?
Real estate inspections are one-time, transaction-focused reports designed to satisfy lenders and buyers. Our checkup is ongoing maintenance—designed to catch problems early, prioritize what needs attention, and help you budget for future repairs. We also don't do invasive testing or code compliance verification.
Will you try to sell me repairs?
Never. Our technicians don't do repairs and aren't paid commission. We have zero financial incentive to exaggerate or upsell. We tell you what we find, prioritize by urgency, and connect you with vetted contractors only if you ask. No pressure, no kickbacks.

Scheduling & Process

How long does the checkup take?
About 90 minutes for most homes. Larger homes or those with more systems (workshop, secondary suite, pool equipment) may take slightly longer. We don't rush—thoroughness is the whole point.
Do I need to be home?
Yes, we ask that someone 18+ be present during the checkup. This lets us point out findings in real-time, answer your questions, and show you exactly what we're looking at. Most homeowners find the walkthrough educational and valuable.
How far in advance do I need to book?
Most checkups are scheduled within 5-7 days of your request. During peak seasons (fall before winter, spring after snowmelt), it may take a bit longer. We offer weekday and Saturday appointments.
How do you inspect the roof with snow on it?
We don't climb on snowy roofs—safety first. For winter bookings, we inspect all interior systems immediately (attic, furnace, plumbing, etc.). Then we return for a complimentary spring visit to inspect the roof, grading, and driveway once the snow melts. Two visits, one price.

Pricing & Payment

How much does it cost?
$249 flat rate for the complete 112-point checkup. No tiers, no add-ons, no surprises. Winter bookings include a free spring follow-up visit.
When do I pay?
After the checkup, not before. We accept credit card, debit, e-transfer, or cash.
What if you don't find anything wrong?
Then the checkup is free—that's our Find-Something Guarantee. But honestly, Calgary homes always have something worth monitoring. Between freeze-thaw cycles, Chinooks, aging systems, and deferred maintenance, we've never left a home without useful findings.
Can I buy this as a gift?
Yes! A House Wellness checkup makes a thoughtful gift for aging parents, new homeowners, or anyone who'd appreciate peace of mind about their home. We offer gift certificates. Learn more →

Service Areas

What areas do you serve?
Calgary and surrounding communities including Airdrie, Cochrane, Chestermere, Okotoks, and most areas within 30 minutes of Calgary. Not sure if you're in our service area? Just ask when you call.
Do you charge extra for areas outside Calgary?
No. The same $249 flat rate applies to Calgary, Airdrie, Cochrane, Chestermere, and Okotoks. No mileage fees or travel charges.

The Report

What do I get after the checkup?
A digital report delivered within 48 hours. It includes photos of every finding, color-coded by priority (green = good, yellow = monitor, red = needs attention), and a prioritized action list so you know what to tackle first.
Can I share the report with contractors?
Absolutely. The report is yours. Many homeowners share it with contractors to get quotes, or keep it as documentation for future reference or resale.
